{{data.wastedVotes}} of {{data.totalVotes}} votes were wasted in {{data.constituency}}. This includes those who voted against their winning MP and surplus votes for the winning MP. In Britain more than half of all voters voted for losing candidates, their votes were simply thrown away.

Wasted votes lead to "tactical voting", where voters don't vote for the candidate they like, but the least worst candidate that may win, over time this leads to a one or two party rule in an area.

Under a Proportional election system like Single Transferable Vote, wasted votes would go to electing MPs the voters want. People who vote against their winning MP (more than half of the electorate in recent times) would have their vote go towards electing a candidate they want.

What this doesn't include:

The data here only talks about the undemocratic nature of the First Past the Post electoral system, it does not cover:

  • An unelected House of Lords with religious clerics, party donors & hereditary power.
  • The big money given to the biggest political parties by corporate donors and trade unions.
  • The effect of a biased politicised media.

The case for reform

Here are just a few of the reasons why our current First Past the Post electoral system is not fit for purpose:

  • Tactical voting, people only vote for one of the two most likely candidates to win in their area in order to prevent their vote being wasted.
  • MPs can be elected on tiny majorities and governments put in power with a little over a third of the popular support.
  • Huge numbers of wasted votes; votes cast for losing candidates count for nothing.
